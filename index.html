EcoTrack

Organisation Overview:

EcoTrack is a environmental startup focused on helping individuals and businesses monitor and reduce their carbon footprint. The organisation promotes sustainable living through digital tools, education, and community engagement. • Mission Statement: To empower people and organisations to make sustainable choices by providing accessible tools that track and reduce environmental impact.
• Vision Statement: To become a leading global platform that drives positive environmental change and promotes a sustainable future for all.
• Target Audience: Environmentally conscious individuals, students, small to medium-sized businesses, and organisations seeking to improve sustainability practices.

Website goals and Objectives:

The primary goal of the EcoTrack website is to provide users with an interactive platform to track their environmental impact.
Objectives include increasing awareness of sustainability, encouraging eco-friendly habits, growing a user community, and generating revenue through premium subscriptions and partnerships.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):

Achieve 10,000 registered users within the first 6 months of launch. Reach a monthly website traffic of 25,000 visitors within 4 months. Maintain a user engagement rate (time on site) of at least 5 minutes per session. Convert 15% of users into active dashboard users (regularly tracking their footprint). Achieve a 5% subscription conversion rate for premium features within 6 months. Maintain a bounce rate below 40%. Gain 3,000 newsletter sign-ups within the first 3 months

Current Website Analysis

Strengths: Simple and clean landing page design. Clear purpose and branding focused on sustainability. Fast loading speed due to minimal content.
Weaknesses: Limited functionality (no user accounts or tracking tools). No mobile responsiveness. Lack of user engagement features (e.g., dashboards, forums). No personalized user experience. Minimal content and lack of educational resources.
Improvements on Weaknesses: Develop interactive features such as user accounts and carbon tracking tools. Implement responsive design to ensure compatibility across all devices. Introduce engagement tools like dashboards, blogs, and community forums. Add personalised features (user goals, progress tracking). Expand content with articles, tips, and sustainability guides to increase value and retention.

Design and User Experience:

Colour Scheme: A nature-inspired palette using shades of green, earthy browns, and soft neutrals to reflect sustainability and environmental awareness. Accent colors like light blue may be used to highlight actions and links.
Typography: Clean and modern fonts such as sans-serif (e.g., Open Sans or Roboto) for readability, with slightly bolder fonts for headings to create visual hierarchy.
Layout and Design: A minimalist, grid-based layout that ensures content is well-organised and easy to navigate. Use of white space to avoid clutter, with clear sections and consistent design elements across all pages.
User Experience Considerations: Mobile-first and fully responsive design. Fast loading times for better performance. Easy navigation with clear menus and buttons. Accessibility features (e.g., readable fonts, contrast, alt text). Personalised user journeys (dashboards and recommendations)

Technical Requirements

The website will be built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript frameworks like React.

Timeline and Milestones:

The project will take 12 weeks: planning (2 weeks), design (3 weeks), development (5 weeks), testing (1 week), and launch (1 week).

Budget:

The estimated budget is R250000, covering design, development, testing, hosting, and maintenance costs.
